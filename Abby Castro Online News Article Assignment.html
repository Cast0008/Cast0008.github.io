Greater Los Angeles Wildfires

Palisades Fire

The first of the three ongoing wildfires to break out in Los Angeles is the Palisades Fire. The Palisades Fire is located in the Santa Monica Mountains. According to CalFire, it grew to be the tenth-deadliest and third-most destructive California wildfire, with eleven deaths and 6,770 destroyed structures. Fortunately, firefighters have made progress and it is now 87% contained.

Eaton Fire

The Eaton Fire was the second one to start and is also still active. It’s located in the Altadena area of Los Angeles County. It is now the fifth deadliest fire in California history, having killed seventeen people total. It’s much deadlier than the Palisades Fire, but it is currently 95% contained.

Hughes Fire

The third and most recent of the fires is the Hughes Fire. The fire began near Lake Hughes Road in Los Angeles County and is ongoing. There has yet to be any recorded deaths and is at 92% containment status.
